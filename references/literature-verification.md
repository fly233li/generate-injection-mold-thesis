# Literature, standards, and citation verification

## Build search groups

Decompose the title into the part, material, geometry features, mold mechanism, gating, cooling, defects, CAE method, and manufacturing. Create Chinese synonyms, English keywords, and grouped searches for each. Search current official standards and primary material/machine/software sources before relying on secondary literature.

## Use the source hierarchy

Prefer:

1. current official standards, manufacturer data sheets, machine catalogs, and software documentation;
2. peer-reviewed full-text articles;
3. authoritative books;
4. theses and conference papers;
5. abstract-only records;
6. search snippets or aggregators only as leads.

CNKI is a discovery and access channel, not automatic proof of quality or claim support.

## Verify CNKI records

1. Use the user's existing signed-in access when available. Do not bypass authentication, institutional access, or payment.
2. Open the actual detail page and store its URL or record ID.
3. Verify title, authors, source, year, volume, issue, pages/article number, and DOI.
4. Export RIS/EndNote/NoteExpress metadata when available, then independently inspect the fields.
5. Record the access level: `metadata-only`, `abstract-read`, or `fulltext-read`.
6. Bind a source only to claims supported at that access level. Detailed formulas, methods, or data require full text and an exact page/section/table/figure locator.
7. Mark unresolved records `unverified`; never infer missing metadata from a similar title or DOI pattern.

For DOI material, cross-check the publisher or Crossref. For Chinese journals, use the journal site when useful. Verify standards on the official national standard platform, material data for the exact commercial grade, and machine values in a manufacturer catalog.

## Manage source states

Use:

`candidate → metadata-verified → abstract-read/fulltext-read → claim-bound → used`

or `rejected` with a reason.

Only `claim-bound` sources enter the formal manuscript. Each used source records claim IDs, manuscript sections, exact locators, and the supported wording. Every in-text citation must have a bibliography entry; every bibliography entry should support an actual in-text claim.

## Render the correct citation standard

Follow the school template first. Without a school rule, verify the currently effective bibliographic standard at project time and record its version. Do not permanently hard-code GB/T 7714-2015 or any other edition. Treat citation-manager exports as metadata drafts and rerender them under the selected standard.

## Prevent citation laundering

- A real article does not automatically support the sentence beside it.
- Do not cite a review for an exact manufacturer value when the primary data sheet is available.
- Do not present a source-recommended range as the source of the designer's chosen value; cite the range and label the selection as a design decision.
- Do not reuse the sample thesis bibliography without reopening and reverifying each record.
- Do not cite an inaccessible full-text result more specifically than the visible abstract permits.
